# Related Work Map

This map keeps only papers that are useful for your next paper choice.

## A. Your direct baseline

### OpenRSD (ICCV 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/ICCV2025/html/Huang_OpenRSD_Towards_Open-prompts_for_Object_Detection_in_Remote_Sensing_Images_ICCV_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It is your baseline.
- It already solves multimodal prompting, dual heads, and multi-stage training.
- Any new paper must go beyond it in a very targeted way.

Main takeaway:

- The remaining room is in semantics, context, and harder cross-domain reasoning, not just prompt quantity.

## B. Remote sensing segmentation is moving fast

### SegEarth-OV (CVPR 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2025/html/Li_SegEarth-OV_Towards_Training-Free_Open-Vocabulary_Segmentation_for_Remote_Sensing_Images_CVPR_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It shows remote sensing open-vocabulary segmentation is already active and competitive.
- It is training-free and broad across 17 datasets.

Main takeaway:

- Segmentation is attractive, but crowded and less directly aligned with your current baseline.

### SCORE (ICCV 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/ICCV2025/html/Huang_SCORE_Scene_Context_Matters_in_Open-Vocabulary_Remote_Sensing_Instance_Segmentation_ICCV_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It shows that **scene context** is highly relevant in remote sensing open-vocabulary recognition.

Main takeaway:

- Context is a strong idea.
- You should port this idea into **rotated detection**, not copy the segmentation task.

## C. General open-vocabulary detection ideas worth borrowing

### SHiNe (CVPR 2024)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2024/html/Liu_SHiNe_Semantic_Hierarchy_Nexus_for_Open-vocabulary_Object_Detection_CVPR_2024_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It proves semantic hierarchy helps open-vocabulary detection.
- It even works with hierarchies generated by large language models.

Main takeaway:

- This is one of the strongest papers supporting your future direction.
- It gives you a principled reason to use LLMs offline for hierarchy construction.

### YOLOE (ICCV 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/ICCV2025/html/Wang_YOLOE_Real-Time_Seeing_Anything_ICCV_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It shows lightweight prompt adaptation and real-time open detection are still very relevant.

Main takeaway:

- Efficiency remains publishable.
- Lightweight prompt modules are acceptable if the results are strong enough.

### The Devil is in the Fine-Grained Details (CVPR 2024)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2024/html/Bianchi_The_Devil_is_in_the_Fine-Grained_Details_Evaluating_Open-Vocabulary_Object_CVPR_2024_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It shows current open-vocabulary detectors are weak on fine-grained detail.

Main takeaway:

- Fine-grained ambiguity is not a solved problem.
- This supports your focus on ships, vehicles, aircraft, and tiny rotated categories.

## D. Evidence that LLMs can help in remote sensing detection

### LLM-Assisted Semantic Guidance for Sparsely Annotated Remote Sensing Object Detection (ICCV 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/ICCV2025/html/Liao_LLM-Assisted_Semantic_Guidance_for_Sparsely_Annotated_Remote_Sensing_Object_Detection_ICCV_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It is direct evidence that LLM-derived semantic priors can improve remote sensing detection.

Main takeaway:

- LLM usage is publishable when it is tied to pseudo-label assignment and semantic priors.
- This is much stronger than an agent-style story.

## E. Foundation model trend in remote sensing

### HyperFree (CVPR 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2025/html/Li_HyperFree_A_Channel-adaptive_and_Tuning-free_Foundation_Model_for_Hyperspectral_Remote_CVPR_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It shows prompt-style and tuning-free foundation model ideas are active in remote sensing.

Main takeaway:

- Prompting/foundation-model thinking is valid, but your paper should remain focused on a concrete downstream task.

### RobSense (CVPR 2025)

Link:

- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2025/html/Kha_RobSense_A_Robust_Multi-modal_Foundation_Model_for_Remote_Sensing_with_CVPR_2025_paper.html

Why it matters:

- It represents the broader foundation-model trend in remote sensing.

Main takeaway:

- The field values robustness and adaptability, not just raw in-domain AP.

## F. My conclusion from the literature

After checking the recent papers, the strongest signal is:

1. **Context matters**
2. **Hierarchy matters**
3. **LLM-derived semantic priors are acceptable when used carefully**
4. **Fine-grained open-vocabulary recognition is still weak**
5. **Agent is not yet a strong benchmark-winning story here**
6. **Diffusion is not the best first bet for your specific task**

That is why the best direction is:

**GeoNexus-RSD = OpenRSD + hierarchy + context + smarter pseudo-label semantics**
